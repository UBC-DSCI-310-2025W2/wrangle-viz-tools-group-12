# Code of Conduct

## Introduction
We are committed to creating a safe, welcoming, and productive environment for everyone. This Code of Conduct applies to all interactions in this repository, including issues, pull requests, and discussions. Our goal is to collaborate effectively through open communication, mutual respect, and positive contributions.

## A Statement on Diversity and Inclusivity
We value diversity and are committed to creating an inclusive environment that respects individuals of all backgrounds, identities, and experiences. This includes differences in race, ethnicity, nationality, gender identity, sexual orientation, religion, ability, age, academic background, and more. We believe that diverse perspectives strengthen our work and lead to better outcomes.

## Expected Behaviour

All participants are expected to:

- Treat others with respect, kindness, and professionalism  
- Communicate openly, honestly, and constructively  
- Be inclusive and considerate of different viewpoints and experiences  
- Provide and receive feedback in a respectful and supportive manner  
- Contribute fairly to the project and meet agreed-upon responsibilities and deadlines  
- Help create a positive, collaborative team environment  

## Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviours are considered unacceptable:

- Harassment, discrimination, or exclusion of any kind  
- Offensive, abusive, or disrespectful language or actions  
- Bullying, intimidation, or personal attacks  
- Disruptive behavior that negatively affects collaboration  
- Plagiarism or any form of academic misconduct  
- Repeated failure to communicate or contribute without valid justification  

## Consequences of Unacceptable Behaviour

If unacceptable behaviour occurs, the following steps may be taken depending on severity:

- Address the issue respectfully within the group  
- Set clear expectations for improvement  
- If the problem continues, escalate the issue to the teaching team  

## Reporting Issues

If a team member experiences or witnesses unacceptable behaviour, they should:

- Raise the concern within the group if appropriate  
- If the issue is serious or unresolved, contact the teaching team for support
